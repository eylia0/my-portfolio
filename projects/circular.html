Circular Economy Concepts

Two redesign concepts applying circular design across infrastructure and consumer products.

Circular Design Repairability Concept Development

Intent

This project explores how design can extend lifespan, reduce waste, and support circular outcomes through reuse, repair, and modular systems — across two different contexts.

What connects both concepts

  • Design for long life, not short replacement cycles
  • Make repair simple, safe, and modular
  • Reduce material waste and enable reuse

Concept 1 — Park Light (Public Infrastructure)

A remodel designed to improve aesthetics while supporting full circularity and ease of maintenance. The system uses recycled LLDPE panels (cut from defective water tanks) and a clipped panel strategy so council workers can replace damaged parts quickly. Long-life LED components reduce maintenance frequency and improve durability. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Concept 2 — Dolphin Torch (Consumer Product)

An improvement proposal that extends product life through reuse and repair. The torch is redesigned to be repurposed into a storage container at end-of-life, includes a replaceable rechargeable battery using Type-C charging, and supports full disassembly with parts sold separately to encourage repair. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Outcome

Two aligned concepts showing how circular design principles scale — from public infrastructure to everyday products — without adding complexity for the user.
