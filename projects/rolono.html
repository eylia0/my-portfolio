Rolo’no Apron

A rolling tool pouch and apron designed for creative professionals — balancing craftsmanship, portability, and material intelligence.

Industrial Design Craft Process

Process

A focused record of decisions — from early layout logic to material testing and refinement.

Ideation

Visual exploration focused on folding behaviour, modular leather systems, and natural unfurling as a way to inform form and interaction.

Visual research → folding logic, modular systems, and behavioural cues.

Pattern & layout tests

Translating early ideas into a workable flat pattern through paper prototyping, scale checks, and layout refinement before committing to material.

Paper testing → proportion, spacing, and functional layout.

Material behaviour

Investigating how leather thickness, stiffness, and forming techniques influenced structure, durability, and edge performance.

Material testing → understanding constraints, response, and tolerance.

Build & detailing

Final construction focused on stitch accuracy, strap integration, and refined detailing to ensure durability and long-term use.

Assembly → precision, durability, and finish.

Design Intent

Rolo’no Apron supports focused, mobile work. The form begins as a flat working surface and transitions into a compact pouch through a single continuous motion — no rigid structure, no excess.

Outcome

A restrained, durable system that feels intuitive in use and refined in appearance — functional first, with one deliberate moment of expression.
