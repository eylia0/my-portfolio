Rolo’no Apron

A rolling tool pouch and apron designed for creative professionals — balancing craftsmanship, portability, and material intelligence.

Industrial Design Craft Process

Process

A focused record of decisions — from early layout logic to material testing and refinement.

Ideation

Early sketches explored tool hierarchy and fold behaviour while keeping the system intuitive.

Sketch exploration → hierarchy + fold logic.

Pattern & layout tests

Template testing helped define pocket spacing, strap placement, and roll direction.

Template testing → proportion + alignment.

Material behaviour

Leather thickness and tension influenced compression, comfort, and durability.

Material testing → tension + softness balance.

Build & detailing

Stitching, edges, and pocket finishing were refined for daily use and visual restraint.

Detail refinement → durability + clean finish.

Design Intent

Rolo’no Apron supports focused, mobile work. The form begins as a flat working surface and transitions into a compact pouch through a single continuous motion — no rigid structure, no excess.

Outcome

A restrained, durable system that feels intuitive in use and refined in appearance — functional first, with one deliberate moment of expression.
